Why Braai and why Vegan?

Braai is about more than making food, it is a social gathering around a fire. There are rituals and etiquette involved and it has a natural rhythm: You light the fire just before the guests arrive, braai is not fast food, it is a ceremony. One of my favourite periods is that time between when the fire has been lit and the coals are ready with everyone just sitting or standing around the fire, drink in hand, catching up, telling jokes, or discussing the players, coaches and game plan of the rugby (or any other sport for that matter). It is important to note that you have to use wood to make the fire, if you use gas or charcoal you are not braaing, you are cooking on gas, which is also fine but not the same. Some of my favourite wood to braai with are Swarthaak in Namibia or Rooikrans in South Africa. Both make good coals and both are invasive alien species so the more we braai the faster we eradicate these threats to biodiversity.

So why Vegan?

I stopped eating meat 4 years ago (See here for the long version or the short version why), but haven't stopped braaing, as explained earlier there is much more to it than making food, and I wasn't prepared to give up those aspects. If there is one way of cooking that is almost synonymous with meat it is a braai, but this need not necessarily be the case. Someone braaing vegetables over the coals is braaing, someone cooking meat on gas is not. What follows below are some fantastic recipes that can be made on a braai.
